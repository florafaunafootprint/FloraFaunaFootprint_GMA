Zone 2 - Playground Beside the Library

Number of Species: 24

Habitat Type: Uneven grassy patch

Plant Types: Perennial trees, herbaceous plants, weeds and grasses.

SPECIES NAME SCIENTIFIC NAME PLANT TYPE HABITAT TYPE COMMONLY FOUND AREAS
Bermuda grassCynodon dactylonPerennial grassSandy shoresExtensively distributed across major regions worldwide. It is native to Africa and has been introduced to various continents including Europe, Asia, and the Americas.
Big blue lilyturfLiriope muscariPerennial or annual herbForests, bamboo forests, scrub, shady, on slopesNative to East Asia, where it grows in forests, scrub areas, and along slopes. Also planted in temperate areas as an ornamental and escaped cultivation in the southeastern United States, South Africa, and Belgium, where it is listed as invasive.
Blue jacarandaJacaranda mimosifoliaPerennial treeTropical, marine, coastal, terrestrialNative to the forests of Bolivia and Argentina, where it is now listed as threatened. It has been introduced sporadically across the globe and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
Carpet-GrassAxonopus compressusPerennial grassRoadsides, GardensPrimarily indigenous to tropical regions of the Americas, spanning Central and South America. Outside its native range, carpet-Grass has been introduced and naturalized across various continents, including Africa, Asia, Oceania, and a few places in Europe.
Chinaberry treeMelia azedarachPerennial treeNature stripNative to South and Southeast Asia including the Himalayan region, cultivated in various locations worldwide. It has been introduced to other continents, often in warmer climates, where it has naturalized in certain areas.
Common cockleburXanthium orientaleAnnual weedExposed slopes, wet lands, moist place, irrigation channelsIndigenous to South America's tropical and subtropical regions. It has spread extensively beyond its native range, and is now found across several continents including Asia, Europe, and parts of Africa.
Common purslanePortulaca oleraceaPerennial or annual weedFields, waste ground, roadside verges, cultivated ground, seasidesA wide native range, consisting of most of the African continent, much of the Middle East and some southern European countries. It has been naturalized almost all over the rest of the world.
Common sunflowerHelianthus annuusAnnual herbPlainsNative to the United States and Mexico, where it grows in prairies and open areas. This plant now has a near global distribution due to its popularity in cultivation.
Dallis grassPaspalum dilatatumPerennial weedLoamy disturbed soils, roadsides, fields, waste places, moist fields or woodsOriginating in South America, it has spread to various regions, often thriving in temperate climates. It is now found in parts of Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Americas, evidencing a broad introduced range.
Elephant bushPortulacaria afraPerennial succulent and shrubThicket, Bushveld, Dry river valleysNative to the south and east coasts of Africa, where it grows along rocky outcrops and dry slopes. It has been introduced in Sicily. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant and, in frost-free regions that include the US southwest, for outdoor landscaping.
Glory bowerVolkameria inermisPerennial tree, vine, and shrub (Its evergreen branches can take the form of a bush or a climber.)Coasts, edges of mangrove forests, saline marshes, swamps, tidal mudNative to tropical regions of South, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands, thriving in areas with warm climates. It has been introduced and naturalized in parts of North Africa and South Asia.
Green amaranthAmaranthus viridisAnnual weedWaste ground, roadsidesA widespread presence, being native to the tropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in a variety of environments. Beyond its native range, green amaranth has been introduced to several continents including Africa, Asia, Europe, and Oceania, indicating its adaptability to diverse climates and conditions.
Kikuyu grassCenchrus clandestinusPerennial grassIn dry and mesic habitatsNative to parts of Eastern Africa, has a significant presence across multiple continents, including Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Oceania. Non-native to Oceania and select parts of Asia and the Americas.
Matted SandmatEuphorbia serpensAnnual weedGardens, yards, orchards, vineyards, crop fields, nursery grounds and containersOriginally native to tropical Americas, thriving in regions from Central to South America. It has since become an introduced species across various continents including Africa, Asia, and parts of Southern Europe. The plant has established itself in both arid and Mediterranean climates.
Neem treeAzadirachta indicaPerennial treeMixed forests, evergreen lowland forestsWidespread cultivation, but primarily native to the Indian subcontinent. It has been introduced to various other regions, flourishing in tropical and subtropical zones across continents. Neem tree is cultivated in areas that mirror its native climate conditions.
Nut grassCyperus rotundusPerennial weedRoadsides, sandy fields, cultivated ground, damp placesWidely dispersed plant with a presence across multiple continents. It is native to various regions, including parts of Asia and Africa, and has been introduced to areas in Europe, North and South America, as well as some islands.
Pellitory of the wallParietaria judaicaPerennial weedHedgebanks, cracks in rocks, dry wallsNative to parts of Eurasia and North Africa, growing on coastal bluffs and in dry or disturbed areas. It has been introduced in scattered spots across North and South America, Australia, and northern Europe.
Prostrate sandmatEuphorbia prostrataAnnual weedDisturbed soils, prairies, pastures, fields, alluvial deposits, roadsides, waste placesNative to most parts of Eurasia, North Africa and South America. It was introduced to other parts of Eurasia, as well as Africa and Australia.
Purple amaranthAmaranthus blitumAnnual weedWastelandsIntroduced worldwide, but originated from Eurasia, South America and Africa. It's considered a weed in some countries in Europe and Asia and in Mozambique and Nigeria. Purple amaranth seems to cause the most trouble in Japan, particularly in warm upland farms.
Shoreline purslaneSesuvium portulacastrumPerennial herbCoastal dunes, beachesIts native distribution encompasses continents such as Asia, Africa, North and South America, and island nations in the Pacific and the Caribbean. It has been introduced to parts of Europe and thrives in cultivated settings, expanding beyond its traditional habitats.
Singapore graveyard flowerPlumeria obtusaPerennial treeOccurs along the coast on rocky or sandy, nutrient poor soils.Natively found in tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean. As a testament to its adaptability and ornamental value, it has been introduced to several countries in Southeast Asia, where it is found both in cultivation and occasionally naturalized. This plant flourishes in warm climates across these continents.
Tropical SignalgrassBrachiaria distachyaVigorous perennial grassDry deciduous forests, wastelands and mangrove forestsGlobally distributed especially in Africa, India, Sri Lanka and Thailand.
White-flowered black mangroveLumnitzera racemosaPerennial treeShorelines such as mangrove Forests, sandy beaches, and rocky beachesEastern Africa to Southeast Asia (including Singapore), Australia, and the Pacific Islands
Whitetip nightshadeSolanum chenopodioidesAnnual or perennial herbDry grasslands, mesic grasslands and open, disturbed placesOriginally South America, now naturalized in Australia, New Zealand, Europe, North America and South Africa. Also, in cultivations, common in areas with human disturbance, between sea level and 1,900 (-2,500) m elevation.
